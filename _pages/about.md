I am a roboticist dedicated to creating truly independent robots. My PhD research focuses on giving robots a sense of "self-understanding" so they can autonomously adapt their behavior to keep working when they or the environment around them changes. Ultimately, my goal is to build systems that don't just shut down when things go wrong, but find safe, creative ways to complete their tasks without needing human rescue.

My work spans a variety of applications, from ensuring long-term reliability for [NASA's space exploration missions](https://www.nasa.gov/reference/jsc-robotics/), to developing safe, contact-aware robots with [Yaashia Gautam](https://yaashia-g.github.io/) at the [ROCC Lab](https://www.colorado.edu/faculty/nicotra/robotics-optimization-and-constrained-control) that can physically interact with their environments without causing harm. I am also a researcher at [Plus One Robotics](https://www.plusonerobotics.com/), there I apply these principles to intelligent package sorting. By giving the system the ability to visually reason about its environment, it can understand when a task fails, independently formulate a recovery plan, and coordinate an entire team of robots and conveyor systems to keep supply chains moving efficiently.

I graduated from The University of Texas at Austin with a Bachelor of Science in Aerospace Engineering in May 2021. I am currently pursuing my Doctor of Philosophy in Computer Science at the **University of Colorado - Boulder** within the [HIRO Group](https://hiro-group.ronc.one/), with an anticipated graduation in May 2027.
